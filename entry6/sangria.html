Sangria

Sangria is a popular and refreshing alcoholic beverage that originated in Spain. It typically consists of red wine, chopped fruit (such as oranges, lemons, and berries), sweeteners, a splash of brandy or another spirit, and sometimes carbonated water or soda. The ingredients are mixed together and then chilled for several hours to allow the flavors to meld. Sangria is known for its fruity and flavorful profile, making it a favorite during warm weather or as a festive party drink. There are many variations of sangria, and recipes can vary based on regional preferences and personal taste. White sangria, made with white wine instead of red, is another common variation. Sangria is often served over ice and garnished with additional fruit slices. It's a versatile drink that can be easily customized to suit different preferences, and it's enjoyed in many countries around the world as a refreshing and social beverage./p>

What's In Sangria?

There are many variations of sangria ingredients, but this recipe for the best sangria calls for: - Red Wine: Opt for a dry, fruity Spanish red wine. - Brandy: Brandy adds an extra sweet and fruity flavor to the sangria. - Triple Sec: Choose a high-quality triple sec for an extra punch of orange flavor. - Fruit Juice:This recipe calls for orange juice, lemon juice, and frozen lemonade concentrate. - Sugar (Optional): If you like your sangria sweeter, add some white sugar. - Carbonated Water (Optional): Add carbonated water for a little fizz, if you like. - Fruit: This recipe calls for sliced lemon, orange, lime, and maraschino cherries.

What Is the Best Fruit For Sangria?

I like to use what this recipe calls for (lemon, orange, lime, and maraschino cherries), but you can really use any in-season fruits that you like.

How Long Does Sangria Last In the Fridge?

Sangria will last for about three to five days in the refrigerator if stored in an airtight container. It will only last for a day or two if stored in the pitcher covered with storage wrap, so I would recommend moving it to an airtight container if you don't plan on drinking it all within two days.

How to Make Sangria

Making the best sangria is so incredibly easy. Simply mix all the liquid ingredients (expect the carbonated water) together in a pitcher, then add the sliced fruit. Refrigerate overnight to allow the flavors to blend. If you're using carbonated water, add it right before serving for the fizziest results.

Ingredients

  • 1 (750 milliliter) bottle dry red wine
  • ½ cup brandy
  • ½ cup triple sec
  • ⅓ cup frozen lemonade concentrate
  • ⅓ cup orange juice
  • ¼ cup white sugar (Optional)
  • 1 medium orange, sliced into rounds
  • 1 medium lemon, sliced into rounds
  • 1 medium lime, sliced into rounds
  • 8 maraschino cherries
  • 2 cups carbonated water (Optional

Directions

Step 1

Gather all ingredients.

Step 2

Mix together red wine, brandy, triple sec, lemonade concentrate, orange juice, lemon juice, and sugar in a bowl. Add orange, lemon, and lime slices and maraschino cherries.

Step 3

Serve immediately over ice, or refrigerate 8 hours to overnight for best flavor. For a fizzy sangria, add club soda just before serving.
